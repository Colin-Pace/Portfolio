Profile

I program single page, interactive, asynchronous web applications that have responsive design for phones, tablets, and computers. The technology that I use is React and Express for the server and client, and PostgreSQL for the database. My first project, Web Text, which provides the opportunity to store notes in folders once the account of a user is registered, authenticated, and authorized, is hosted on Heroku. I wrote my second project, Minesweeper, in plain JavaScript. It is hosted on GitHub pages. Next I am going to write a paint program that allows users to branch the creations of others, in order to create a new work. Its technology will be React, Express, and PostgreSQL.

In addition to these projects, I have written, translated from Java, or in a few instances adapted from JavaScript more than 150 algorithms for common problems of technical interviews. The data structures of the algorithms range from arrays and linked lists to stacks and graphs. I found the prompts of the problems in different places, like Cracking the Coding Interview, LeetCode, HackerRank, and freeCodeCamp. The algorithms are collected in a repository on my GitHub account. In another repository there, are my implementations of introductory programs of neural networks. I found the programs in various tutorials on YouTube. I wrote them in Python. Other projects on GitHub include an adaptation of a web crawler in JavaScript and a series of recreations of the front end of websites. I plan to recreate the pages that are on the Alexa top sites in the United States.

I have a 2017 MacBook Air that has the Mojave operating system. I use the Atom text editor. My phone is an LG Tribute Dynasty.

I began to learn web development in 2018. At the time, I was a graduate student at The University of Texas at Austin in The Department of Anthropology. I had studied a number of languages at UT. They include Arabic, French, Hindi, Urdu, Chinese, Burmese, and Latin. As a child and teenager, I had studied Spanish, my first second language. Of the languages I studied at the university, I took the most courses in Hindi, and also studied it for four semesters at The American Institute of Indian Studies in Jaipur, Rajasthan. With world class instruction and a plethora of experiences in diverse grammars and vocabularies, spanning the Afro-Asiatic, Sino-Tibetan, and Indo-European family trees, I found myself at a crossroads in The Department of Anthropology with a methodological conundrum.

The conundrum was whether I should pursue the discipline of anthropology or another that is more focused on language. Because of my deep and abiding passion for syntax and morphology, in 2018, as an exploration into a potential new direction, I began to learn to program. I started with Python and immediately enjoyed it immensely. After working through a few textbooks, I changed languages, learning the syntax of JavaScript, HTML, and CSS. I started to read Java and C++. With these experiences, I was convinced that I wanted to become a programmer. I left UT and enrolled in the remote course for web development at Thinkful Bloc. There I learned the technology for programming single page, interactive, asynchronous web applications with responsive design for multiple devices.

In late 2019, I visited Seattle and attended a few MeetUps. Having participated in and observed the web development community there, as well as having attended a few job fairs, I traveled to Reston, Virginia to spend a week at the coding bootcamp Revature. Since the curriculum there concerns Java and my interest and training are in JavaScript, I then returned to Austin in early 2020, where I had grown up, to look for opportunities in web development. I am continuing the search in spite of the anomalous and frightening pandemic. I am also refining my programs, and writing new programs and algorithms, while renewing a study of Hindi, French, and Spanish.
