Vocabulary


Term Definition

immunogenetics medicine of relation between immune system and genetics
BH3 mimetics molecular mimicry of BH3-proteins: bind to apoptotic BCL-2 proteins
p53 protein if bound to DNA it creates p21 and cdk2, preventing cell division; on chromosome 17
lipid peroxidation the oxidative degradation of lipids by free radicals stealing electrons from the lipids in a cellular membrane
hydroperoxide GPX4 an enzyme coded by the GPX4 gene for lipid peroxidation
RAS/MAPK signaling the best characterized signal transduction pathway in cellular biology; it transduces signals from outside the cell to the nucleus for division and differentiation

adjuvant therapy applied after initial treatment, for secondary tumor formation
cytotoxic therapy anticancer drugs that inhibit cell division
natural killer cells a type of lymphocyte that rejects cells with tumors and viruses
CD8+T mediators of adaptive immunity, including types of T cells

progeria a rare genetic condition that causes premature aging
redox homeostasis the endogenous capacity of a cell to adapt to electrophile generation
NAD+ nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide: a coenzyme that plays a role in hundreds of metabolic processes
nicotinamide riboside a variation of vitamin B3
nicotinamide mononucleotide a derivative of niacin, it is used by humans to generate NAD

PARPis PARP polymerase inhibitors
homologous recombination nucleotide sequences are exchanged between DNA

peritoneal the serous membrane in the lining of the abdomen

proteome the entire complement of proteins that can be expressed

etiology the cause of a disease
pathophysiology the processes associated with a disease
renal cell kidney cell
organoid an artificially grown mass of cells that resembles an organ
high-throughput sequencing parallel sequencing of hundreds of millions of DNA molecules
HSP90 a chaperone protein that assists others in folding, heat stress, and degradation

Ideas

1. Investigations into cancer started 40 years ago, with the discovery of oncogenes.

2. Half of all human cancers contain TP53 mutations, and in many others, the function of the protein is compromised.

3. The hallmarks and susceptible processes of ferroptosis, make it an attractive target for precision cancer medicine.

4. Since tumors gain adaptive immunity over blocking antibodies, so immunotherapy looks for other strategies, including those outside endogenous immunity: vaccines, adjuvants, and cytotoxic therapy are examples.

5. Natural killer cells complement T cell responses because of the former do not lose MHC class I.

6. Because decline of the signaling molecule NAD+ is common in cancers related to aging, therefore administration of the molecule's nicotinamide precursors is a promising therapeutic strategy.

7. Recently PARPis have been approved for the treatment of ovarian and breast cancers with BRCA mutations, and more players beyond homologous recombination are emerging as response determinants to PARPis.

8. Although traditionally genomic and transcriptomic methods have profiled the intricate ecosystems of tumors in bulk samples, where cancer and noncancer cells interact, nonetheless recent advances in single-cell profiling have paved the way for studying tumors at the resolution of individual cells, providing insights into drug resistance, stem cell programs, metastasis, and classification.

9. Genomic sequencing reveals that cancerous cells have aberrant RNA splicing, which thereby becomes a target for treatment.

10. Cell-free DNA allows the assessment of the level and composition of tumor DNA from a routine, noninvasive blood draw, complementing the method of biopsy.

11. Because cancerous cells have an increased demand for energy, therefore when nutrients become a limitation, then the cells rely on nonconventional energy sources, relying on activation of pathways such at the MiT/TFE family of transcription factors.

12. Organoid cultures are powerful model systems for discovery.

13. Although PARP have only recently entered the clinic for the treatment of HR deficient cancers, nonetheless resistance to the inhibitors is a hurdle; therefore it is worth exploring new synthetic dependencies as sites to exploit for cells resistant to targeted therapies.

14. Starting in the 1970s, the question of whether dietary consumption of sugar and fat causes cancer, continues today.

15. Although the stability and function of many oncogenic mutant proteins depend on heat shock protein 90, nonetheless because cancer is adaptable and heterogenous, and also because of their aberrant and foreign proteome to the immune system, therefore the most effective use of HSP90 might not be at high doses with the intent to kill cancer, but in combination with other targeting therapies that limit cancerous adaptation.

16. Recent advances in massively parallel sequencing reveal that a third of soft tissue sarcomas carry recurrent and characteristic chromosomal translocations.

17. Because the cGAS-STING pathway plays a critical role during infection, and because cGAS detects tumor derived DNA and stimulates endogenous antitumor immunity, therefore it is a promising target for cancer immunotherapy.

18. Functional genomics is a promising field of cancer biology.

19. Although mutation that results in the chronic activation of signaling pathways drives human cancer, nonetheless since the epigenetic state of a cell dictates the response to an oncogenic signal, therefore oncogenic and epigenetic defects together create novel therapeutic vulnerabilities that can be targeted by drug combinations.

20. The status quo of anticancer drug development between the academy and industry, fails to create late-stage drugs, but is otherwise in a golden age.

21. Because neoplastic cells co-opt preexisting or treatment-induced signaling networks to survive chemotherapy, therefore disease recurrence following treatment remains an intractable clinical problem and a major impediment to reducing the mortality attributable to malignant tumors.

22. DLBCL have genetic and phenotypic heterogeneity.

23. Recent discoveries reveal that the FA pathway functions in a critical tumor-suppressor network to preserve genomic integrity by stabilizing replication forks, mitigation replication stress, and regulating cytokinesis.
