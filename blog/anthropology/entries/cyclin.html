Ideas

1. Autophagy can constrain tumors in normal tissue, and can also maintain established tumors, providing them with metabolism and drug resistance.

2. Mutated oncogenes and inactivated tumor suppressor genes can increase the dependency of cancerous cells on G1-phase cyclin dependent kinases, augment replication stress and DNA damage during S phase, and dismantle checkpoints that monitor progression through S/G2/M.

3. The APC/C and SCF bound to F-box proteins, regulate fundamental proteins that are associated with cancer.

4. To hyperactivate the cell signaling pathways of tumorigenesis, cancerous cells increase the rate of ROS production in comparison with the rate of normal cells. In order to maintain ROS homeostasis and evade cell death, they also increase the antioxidant capacity.

5. The toolkit for studying the hallmark metabolic features of tumors in vivo, include metabolomics, positron emission tomography, magnetic resonance spectroscopy, and multiparametric magnetic resonance imaging.

6. Cancerous mutation is upregulated by stress response.

7. Synthetic lethality suggests that a cancerous mutation gives rise to new therapeutic vulnerabilities.

8. The most commonly studied ncRNA species is microRNAs, which are included in the tumor suppressor network and which have been used as biomarkers and therapeutic reagents.

9. Among all p53 alterations identified in human cancers, p53 missense mutations predominate. The mutant p53 retains a potent, indirect transcriptional activation domain.

10. A common difficulty among the types of cancerous therapy is that populations of replicating cells move away from opposing selection pressure and evolve resistance. The evolution of resistance can be modeled mathematically.

11. Evolutionary forces direct tumorous development.

12. Although blocking inhibitory receptors on T cells, 'checkpoints', results in durable clinical responses, nonetheless because a majority of patients do not respond to the therapy, therefore the challenge is to understand the tissue-protective cellular elements of the tumor microenvironment that exclude T cells from the vicinity of cancer cells.

13. Genotype guided therapy is becoming increasingly common. Tyrosine kinases are attractive targets, but resistance still develops from genetic alteration in the kinase target that allows cancerous cells to escape the TKI mediated growth suppression.

14. Cancerous cells engage the apoptotic machinery, regulated by the BCL-2 family of proteins that control the permeabilization of the mitochondrial outer membrane, more than do normal cells. Recently, ABT-199, a small molecule that targets the antiapoptotic proteins of the BCL-2 family, have gained FDA approval.

15. Mouse models of chemical carcinogens led to the concept of multistage tumor development through distinct stages of initiation, promotion, and progression. Recent breakthroughs in DNA sequencing allow the complete dissection of the tumor genome. It has been learned that chemical cancers in mice, have a high point mutation load.

16. Solid tumors are characterized by a remodeled and stiffened extracellular matrix that enhances proliferation and migration and influences stromal cells to promote tumorous progression.

17. Approximately 90 percent of tumors have gained or lost at least one chromosome. It occurs about as often as disruption of the p53 pathway.

18. Recently small molecule inhibitors have been tested for recurrent somatic mutations that are associated with chromatin proteins.

19. Mutation in the IDH genes, produces 2-HG. The accumulation of 2-HG induces hypermethylation of histones and DNA. Such methylation blocks cellular differentiation. Clinical tests are underway to inhibit IDH mutation.

20. Metastasis is determined by a host of factors. They include blood flow and the environment of the target organ. Aspects of the environment are vascular architecture, stromal cell content, and growth factors, signaling molecules, and metabolic substrates that are permissive or antagonistic to metastatic colonization. Furthermore, metastatic cells posses properties for adaptation in environments where they co-opt growth and survival signals, undergo metabolic reprogramming, and subvert resident stromal cell activities to promote extravasation, immune evasion, angiogenesis, and overt metastatic growth. Recent work has revealed mediators that prepare metastatic niches.

21. The methyltransferase activities of MLL and other family members, function in diverse macromolecular complexes called complex of proteins associated with Set1.

22. Two recent advances in immunotherapy are the blocking of immune inhibitory checkpoints and the engineering of T lymphocytes. CARs successfully target CD19 in B cell malignancies. They redirect and activate T cells and reprogram their effector, metabolic, and survival functions, enabling the rapid manufacture of tumor specific agents. The previous paradigm of cellular therapy relied on the identification and expansion of rare naturally occurring T cells.
