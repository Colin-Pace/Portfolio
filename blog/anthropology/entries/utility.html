When multiple opportunities are logical, a community decides which to pursue.

For example, in 2019 the US federal budget was some 4.5 trillion dollars. Most of it is spent on care for the elderly, the impoverished, and the ill. The military takes a sizable share. The rest is devoted to programs and research. This is a logical division of the taxes, since those are values of the national community. Another logical division, would be to divert a greater portion of the outlay to scientific research that could prevent the disease, poverty, and conflict that costs so much.

There are attempts to define an optimal logic. Bentham and Mill suggested utilitarianism, the idea that if multiple logics are at play, the greatest good, for them characterized by the debatable notion of happiness, should be the guide in selection among them. Jefferson and the others at the Second Continental Congress agreed to some extent, in the pursuit of happiness.

I was surprised to learn that the top 10 percent of income earners pay about 70 percent of the taxes, and the top 50 percent of income earners pay about all the taxes. Also surprising is that although about 80 million people pay nearly all the taxes, 200 million adults have the opportunity to vote.
