Vocabulary


Term Definition

carfilzomib a selective proteasome inhibitor, for multiple myeloma

E3 ubiquitin ligase regulation of cell traffic, DNA repair, and signaling

RAS protein inter cellular signal transmission; of small GTPase family
von Hippel-Lindau tumor suppressor two-hit suppressor, linked to cancers of the kidney and nervous system
hemangioblastomas benign tumors that arise from blood vessel lining
sympathoadrenal connection between sympathetic NS and the adrenal medulla; regulates an organism's response to outside stimuli
paragangliomas rare tumors near the carotid artery or along nerve pathways in the head and neck
pVHL a protein that with HIF mediates oxygen homeostasis
cullin a family of hydrophobic scaffold protein for ubiquitin ligase
hypoxia inducible factor (HIF) transcription factors that respond to decreases in available oxygen in cellular environments
VEGF vascular endothelial growth factor: a signaling protein that promotes the growth of new blood vessels
circadian molecular clock regulates and coordinates cellular metabolism

BMAL1 also known as ARNTL; a protein that encodes a transcription factor with a basic helix loop helix and two PAS domains

immune/inflammatory cascade activation of macrophages rapidly increases their number

prophylactic intended to prevent disease
colorectal relating to the colon and rectum
microbiota the microorganisms of a particular site
genotoxicity property of chemical agents to damage genes and cause potentially cancerous mutations
nutrient responsive transcription factors include: HIF, SREBP, ATF

ATF activating transcription factor
signaling pathways include: mTORC1, AMPK

hormone a regulatory substance transported by tissue fluids; they stimulate specific cells into action

aldehyde -CHO compound

Ideas

1. Unorthodox career paths might lead to scientific discoveries.

2. Recent studies have shown that insulated neighborhoods can contribute to oncogene dysregulation.

3. The ubiquitin-proteasome system regulates protein homeostasis, and the blockade of the system results in a collection of anti-cancerous proteins; preclinical data about PROTACs suggest a new approach to target currently undruggable proteins.

4. Recent studies have shown that the Hippo pathway translates cellular cues into transcriptional responses by receiving input from a wide range of upstream signals.

5. Because RAS protein has no pocket to which a drug could bind with high affinity, therefore even though it plays a major, causal role in many cancers, still no therapies have been developed for it. New technologies such as nuclear magnetic based fragment screening and covalent tethering, as well as insight into the structure of RAS protein, have made attachment more likely, prompting the development of several drug candidates.

6. The gene product of VHL is pVHL, and it targets HIF, which is implicated in the pathogenesis of pVHL-defective tumors; new treatments attempt to inhibit the HIF responsive gene.

7. CRISPR/Cas9 genome editing can quickly and precisely manipulate the genome.

8. The mammalian clock is a circuitry of transcription factors anchored by BMAL1-CLOCK, and its disruption by for example jet lag or shift work, heightens cancer risk and accelerates tumorigenesis.

9. Recently an oHSV type 1-encoding GM-CSF demonstrated clinical benefit for advanced melanoma. Then it was given regulatory approval in 2015.

10. Senescent cells can have tumor suppressive or pro-oncogenic effects on adjacent cancer cells and other players in the tumor microenvironment such as the stroma, vasculature, and immune system.

11. Although development of prophylactic cancer vaccines for oncogenic viruses have been demonstrated, nonetheless because most human cancers are not caused by viruses, therefore novel approaches are needed for the development of prophylactic vaccines for nonviral cancers.

12. Mechanisms of tumor cell primary and secondary immune evasion, which resist checkpoint blockade therapy, include the following two: the tumor establishes equilibrium during T cell infiltration; exclusion of the T cell from the tumor microenvironment.

13. In addition to genetic mutation, the alteration of gut microbiota composition can influence cancer incidence and progression.

14. By responsive transcription factors, tumors tune their metabolic output according to the availability of nutrients, thereby balancing tumorous proliferation with anabolic response. They also recycle and scavenge to collect nutrients: such processes are called autophagy and macropinocytosis.

15. There are similarities between lineage plasticity and tumorigenesis. They are both caused by severe tissue damage, inflammation, and senescence. Another connection is that drivers of cellular plasticity include oncogenic suppressor pathways. Such inclusion suggests that cancer cells might use plasticity to evade detection and treatment.

16. Recently there have been discoveries relating to ER and AR signaling in the presence of antihormal agents, which enable the loss of hormone dependency and provide multiple routes of acquired resistance to hormone therapy.

17. Recent advances in our understanding of BRCA1 and BRCA2 include how they interact with repair factors, protect stalled replication forks, affect the response of aldehydes, and link to mutation signatures if they are lost.

18. Recent descriptions of recurrent driver mutations in the genes encoding histones, of which the most common alteration is the substitution of methionine to lysine on H3.3K27 and H3.3K36, also exhibit exclusive expression in specific cell lineages and alter the epigenetic landscape of tumors, leading to a blockade of differentiation.

19. A new understanding of proteolysis, has led to next generation in diagnostics as a cancerous biomarker and in therapy as its modulation in concert with secondary targets.

20. Because metastatic lesions can become symptomatic years or decades after primary tumor removal, therefore DTCs are not immediately competent to initiate growth and persist in dormancy. Three scenarios that result in dormancy are instruction from the organ of the microenvironment, pre-encoding from primary tumors, and cancerous similarity with the normal dormancy instructing signals.

21. Extracellular vesicles mediate intercellular communication through ligand-receptor interactions and membrane fusion at the surface of or within recipient cells.

22. Recent discoveries reveal that SWI/SNF chromatin complexes control the transcription of lineage-specific differentiation. These subunits are major targets of cancerous mutation.

23. The catalog of DNA alteration that results in tumorigenesis, has been elucidated by massively parallel sequencing and computational analysis methods. The catalog gives insight into mutational activation and inactivation of cellular pathways, resulting in the growth and division of normal and cancerous cells.
