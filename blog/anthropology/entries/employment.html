I am looking for my first full time position. I was a student at The University of Texas at Austin from 2006 through 2018. I have an undergraduate degree in Cultural Anthropology, an MA in Asian Cultures and Languages, and enough credits for a PhD in either department, but no degree because of a difference of perspective with the faculty in both departments. My MA thesis is an essay about the Hindi language and Indian history, which is hosted at UT's library. I have a National Science Foundation scholarship, and a few Foreign Language and Area Studies scholarships from the Department of Education. I am an excellent researcher, writer, and speaker, and I am comfortable working in a number of languages.

During 2019 and 2020, I learned programming. My focus is web development and the composition of efficient algorithms with data structures. An example project I've made is called Web Text, a website where you can write and store text in folders and posts. It's at webtext.xyz.

I'm having difficulty getting an interview. I'm not sure why. I am capable for an entry level position in client and server development, and both combined. I can research markets and write reports. Because I spent so much time at UT, therefore I at 33 years old am perhaps older than most candidates seeking their entry level position. The university is so far zero help. The pandemic is not helping.

I wonder how I am supposed to enter the workforce. Some have suggested taking temporary positions such as in a warehouse. While that is good work for the moment, it does not seem like a stepping stone for the future. With ample skill, what I need is an opportunity to prove myself and gain experience.
