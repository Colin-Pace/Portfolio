The achievements of the scientific method

What gives me confidence and serenity is science. The method, first given by Aristotle and then refined by many including Bacon, Newton, and Popper, is unique for humanity. Science is the first to suggest that the reproduction of experimentation is the optimal process whereby knowledge about nature can be created and comprehended.

With the method, humans have discovered the origin of the universe and its topology. For example, we know the levels of matter. Three quarks are in a proton. Protons and neutrons form atoms. Molecules comprise atoms. Molecules constitute cells. Cells combine in organisms. Organisms live in populations. Ecosystems sustain populations. Biospheres contain ecosystems. A planet has biospheres. A solar system contains planets. Billions of solar systems are in a galaxy. Galaxies drift apart along a filament of a web like structure throughout the universe. The horizon of the universe moves faster than the speed of light, perhaps driven by accumulations of dark matter and energy that are measurable but otherwise not discernible. The precision and hopefully accuracy of this picture is awesome.

The scientific method also tells us about the place of humanity in nature. The fossil record, morphological resemblances among taxa, DNA comparisons, and observation of microevolution and perhaps even speciation are evidence of the evolution of humanity. These techniques offer also a story about the history of humanity on Earth. Before mammalia, there were reptiles and others, dinosaurs, aquatic life in the Cambria, and cellular life before then. At no point in the story, is the justification for a character or a plot turn a desire of the storyteller. It was written by nature. Humans only have to use the scientific method to read the story.

The disciplines of science have formed within the last few centuries. Based on the sizes of the objects that are the basic units of the disciplinary analyses, one might posit physics and the atom, chemistry and the molecule, biology and the cell, geology and the planet, and astronomy and the star as the natural sciences. Combinations such as geophysics and molecular biology bridge the disciplines.

These disciplines of science give me confidence because the requirements of the scientific method are those which humanity has already achieved. Since we have done them before, repetition proves a question not of possibility but patience during refinements of technique, and creativity during moments of discovery. And they give me serenity because the continual achievements of the scientific method indicate balance and continuity for the future. Such progress suggests that civilizational problems like cancer and climate change, are not permanent, but impermanent.
