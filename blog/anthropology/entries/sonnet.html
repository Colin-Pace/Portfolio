18 May 2020

What does not replicate on its own,
Coated by protein on a destructive bent,
And must briefly use a cell for its home,
A virus is acid with ill intent;
The cycle binds, enters, and creates,
Assembles, releases, and spreads,
Genetic copies of itself,
Through the body, in its wake the hosts left dead;
There is no cure but only prevention,
An antiviral that targets a phase,
To stop the intruder or its recreation,
A glaring lacuna in the modern age;
Who can find a cure, who can stop the wrath,
Who can end the sickness, who can bring us back?
