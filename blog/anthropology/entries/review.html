Ideas

1. AMPK prevents tumors before, but accelerates them after tumorigenesis.

2. Stromal cells in a tumor microenvironment contribute to tumorous metabolism.

3. Major pathways that control mitophagy include PINK1 and BNIP3/NIX. Signal mechanisms that sense mitochondrial dysfunction, activate mitophagy; defective mitophagy results in cancer.

4. Targeting protein interaction by chemicals could stop MYC tumors.

5. The mutation of mitochondrial enzymes, predisposes a person to familial cancer.

6. Mouse models can interrogate tumor heterogeneity.

7. Synthetic modules could be used for solid cancers. Aside from CAR T cells for treating B cell malignancies, most T cell therapies are risky, toxic, and ineffective.

8. Reduction of tumor acidity contributes to activation of the antitumor immune response.

9. Reactivated retroelements both drive tumorigenesis and potentiate immune checkpoint blockades and cancer vaccines.

10. The WNT pathway is activated in multiple cancers.

11. EMT regulators participate in pancreatic and breast cancer models.

12. Aberrant RNA modifications contribute to human cancers. Over 170 chemical modifications are in protein-coding and noncoding RNAs.

13. In recent tests, adding epacadostat to the reference pembrolizumab, had a negative result, but the IDO1 pathway remains an attractive target for immunotherapy.

14. Oncoviruses take control of human cell division by exploiting the SAC. The signaling circuitry of the SAC might be a pool of drug targets for developing effective therapy.

15. Multifaceted and simultaneous acquired resistance is the major cause of mortality for lung cancer patients.

16. Small-molecule inhibitors of the antiapoptotic MCL-1 are undergoing clinical testing.

17. Nongenetic primary resistance mechanisms, create and maintain persister melanoma populations.

18. There is little understanding of the immune response and resistance to ICB; biomarkers could help, and a few are currently in development.

19. The recently credentialed immune based therapy of childhood cancers allows a cure for those with previously lethal disease.

20. The role of the nervous system in cancer remains largely unlearned.

21. About 80 percent of metastatic cancer patients suffer from cachexia, which often renders them too weak to tolerate standard doses of anticancer therapies and makes them susceptible to death from cardiac and respiratory failure.

22. Cancer is dependent on DNU and ROS signaling pathways, though types of cancer vary in their utilization of them.

23. Cancer cells modulate the translation machinery to adapt to oncogenic stress.

24. Insights into the biology of Treg cells that promote tumor development and dampen antitumor immune responses, have created an opportunity for targeting cancer.
