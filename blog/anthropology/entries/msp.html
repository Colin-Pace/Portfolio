Here is a list of some of the works that created the modern scientific paradigm. After the title of the work, I write my characterization of their contribution.

  • On the revolutions of the heavenly spheres; heliocentrism (1543) Nicolaus Copernicus

  • Mathematical principles for natural philosophy; the laws of motion, gravity, and calculus (1687) Isaac Newton

  • Opitcs; the spectrum of light (1704) Isaac Newton

  • The theory of Earth; the rock cycle (1788) James Hutton

  • Elementary treatise of chemistry; chemical reaction for phase change (1789) Antoine Lavoisier

  • Stoichiometry or measurement of chemical elements; equations for molecular reactions (1792) Jeremias Richter

  • Principles of geology; uniformitarianism (1833) Charles Lyell

  • On the origin of species by means of natural selection; evolution (1859) Charles Darwin

  • On physical lines of force; electromagnetism (1861) James Maxwell

  • Experiments on plant hybridization; genetics (1866) Gregor Mendel

  • The dependence between the properties of the atomic weights of the elements; the periodic table of elements (1869) Dimitri Mendeleev

  • The age of the Earth; the geologic timescale, updated by C. Patterson in 1956 (1918) Arthur Holmes

  • A spiral nebula as a stellar system, Messier 31; galaxies outside the Milky Way (1926) Edwin Hubble

  • The primeval atom; expansion, verified by Hubble (1931) Georges Lemaitre

  • On the masses of nebulae and of clusters of nebulae; dark matter (1937) Franz Zwicky

  • Molecular structure of nucleic acids: A structure for deoxyribose nucleic acid; the double helix (1953) JD Watson and FHC Crick

  • A planetary system around the millisecond pulsar; exoplanets (1992) A Wolszczan and DA Frail

  • Observational evidence from supernovae for an accelerating universe and a cosmological constant; dark energy (1998) Riess et al.

  • A new boson at a mass of 1.25 GeV with the CMS experiment at the LHC; Higgs boson (2012) Abbaneo et al.
