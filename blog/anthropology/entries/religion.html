It is they, the coincidences, that many people take as signs of the divine that bolster faith.

Without intending disrespect, I am interested in the boundary line between delusion and religion. Religious buildings and discourse allow people to express belief in what is not demonstrable, to structure their lives and community around the belief, and even to marginalize those who doubt the sensibility of the belief. In what other circumstance could someone stand up and sing praises of a force that governs omnisciently and omnipotently? Outside religious contents, civilizations lock people up for that behavior. Yet a majority of people profess faith.

In addition to the illogical nature of religion, which respectfully is to say the faith of religion, there are societal encrustations that build on it such as the drain of wealth, time, and attention from communities. There could be a lot more doctors and engineers. Violence performed in the name of religion is clearly deleterious.

The function of religious discourse is more sensible. People desire stories about the afterlife. It is easier to believe that there is a transcendental warrant for the claim of morality than to derive an abstraction of it from mundane circumstances that can then be imperfectly implemented in future contexts. Despite these redemptions, the transition between rationality and faith in society, whether in conversation or space, makes me sad about humanity, even if a profound humanism is part of the philosophy that inspires faith in a religion and allows the apparent mental compartmentalization.

Sometimes I wonder if the coincidences that I perceive after prayer are alien interventions in my psychic life. It is they, the coincidences, that many people intrepret as signs of the divine that bolster faith. They are often so subtle and succinct that if one is an intention, then the causative power is so great indeed that it is beyond the faculty of understanding of our species. Whether there are alien human hybrids that enact the coincidences or whether a presence coordinates its intention through people, is another recurrent question, and one that is beyond comprehension and nonetheless continually itchy.
